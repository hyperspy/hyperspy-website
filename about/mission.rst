Mission and Principles
======================

Mission
-------

To make multi-dimensional data analysis intuitive, reproducible, and accessible
to all by developing tools of the highest quality, maximizing their benefit to
the scientific community and to society.

Principles
----------

The following principles guide how we pursue our mission.

Open-source
^^^^^^^^^^^

Open development produces better software and maximizes its benefit to society.
HyperSpy's source code, documentation, and development process are fully open,
ensuring transparency and enabling anyone to study, modify, and redistribute
the software.

Community-driven
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

The best tools are shaped by the people who use them. HyperSpy is guided by
the collective wisdom of its users and developers. Decisions are made
collaboratively, and contributions from all members of the community are
valued and encouraged.

User friendly and inclusive
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

The easier a tool is to use, the greater its impact. Comprehensive
documentation, an intuitive syntax, and low barriers to participation ensure
that HyperSpy is usable by anyone, regardless of background, discipline,
or level of programming experience.

Reproducible
^^^^^^^^^^^^

Scientific progress depends on results that can be verified and built upon.
HyperSpy's scripting-first design ensures that analyses are transparent,
repeatable, and shareable.

High-performance
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

Modern scientific datasets demand tools that scale with them. HyperSpy is
designed to be powerful, scalable, and efficient, meeting the demands of
research from routine analysis to cutting-edge investigations.

Adaptable
^^^^^^^^^

HyperSpy's modular, extensible
architecture allows it to be tailored for specific domains and workflows
through community-developed extensions.
